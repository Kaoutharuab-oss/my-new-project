# my-new-project
Building AI course project
Optimizing Retail Employee Well-being via AI
AI Building Course Project

**Summary**
An AI-driven retail management system designed to optimize shift scheduling and workload forecasting. It balances commercial KPIs with labor health standards, mandatory rest periods, and work-life balance, effectively reducing frontline employee burnout.

**Background**
In the retail sector, work schedules are often subject to high variability, rotating shifts, and intense pressure to meet sales targets or KPIs. This frequently leads to high stress levels and significant difficulties in balancing work with personal life or university studies.

The motivation behind this project is to demonstrate that machine learning and technology should not solely be used to maximize corporate profit. Instead, AI can serve as a key tool to ensure compliance with labor regulations (such as the Workers' Statute), optimize the prevention of psychosocial risks, and genuinely improve the working environment for retail staff in our local communities.

**Data and AI Techniques**
To function effectively, the system relies on the following data sources while strictly respecting privacy and data protection regulations:

Historical sales and foot traffic data: To accurately predict peak hours in the store.

Collective bargaining agreements and calendars: To integrate official holidays, maximum annual working hours, and mandatory minimum rest periods between shifts.

Employee preferences and constraints: To incorporate university class schedules, preferred rest days, and previous fatigue logs.

**Proposed AI Techniques:**
Linear Regression / Time Series Forecasting: To accurately predict customer influx and store staffing needs, preventing unexpected workload spikes.

Constrained Optimization Algorithms: To automatically generate shift schedules where labor health, legal regulations, and work-life balance constraints carry the same strategic weight as commercial targets.

**How is it used?**
The solution is designed to be integrated into existing workforce management platforms and corporate applications used within the retail sector (such as Quinyx or similar tools).

Primary Users: Store managers and Human Resources departments, who will receive schedule proposals that are already ethically and legally optimized.

Affected Stakeholders: Sales assistants and frontline retail workers, who will benefit from a much more predictable, fair, and respectful planning of their work and personal or academic lives.

**Challenges**
This project faces clear limitations that an AI system cannot solve on its own:

Unpredictable Human Factors: Last-minute sick leaves or emergency absences that fundamentally require human empathy and manual intervention to resolve.

Corporate Adoption Barrier: Convincing corporate leadership that investing in employee well-being is highly profitable in the long run by significantly reducing absenteeism and turnover.

Initial Data Quality: If past time-tracking logs or shift records contain errors or systematic biases, the AI might perpetuate deficient scheduling patterns.

**What's next?**
The immediate next step for this project would be to develop a small-scale prototype to be tested in a controlled environment, such as a single pilot store. In the long term, this system could evolve by incorporating Natural Language Processing (NLP) models so that employees can easily communicate their scheduling and scheduling flexibility needs through a virtual assistant integrated directly into their work app.
